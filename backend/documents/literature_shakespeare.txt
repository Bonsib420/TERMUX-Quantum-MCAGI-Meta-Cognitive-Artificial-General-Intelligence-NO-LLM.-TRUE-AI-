William Shakespeare was an English playwright and poet, widely regarded as the greatest writer in the English language. His works include 39 plays, 154 sonnets, and three long narrative poems. His plays are categorized as tragedies (Hamlet, Othello, King Lear), comedies (A Midsummer Night's Dream, Much Ado About Nothing), and histories (Henry V, Richard III). Shakespeare invented thousands of words and phrases still used today. His themes explore human nature, power, love, jealousy, and ambition. The Globe Theatre in London housed many of his performances. His writing employs iambic pentameter and rich imagery. Shakespeare's works remain globally performed and studied. They offer insights into Elizabethan society and universal human conditions. His influence extends beyond literature into language, psychology, and culture.
