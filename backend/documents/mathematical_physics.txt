Mathematical physics applies rigorous analysis to physical problems. Differential equations describe motion and fields. Linear algebra underpins quantum mechanics. Fourier transforms solve wave equations. Probability theory models statistical mechanics. Symmetry principles lead to conservation laws. Tensor calculus generalizes Newton's laws to curved spacetime. The Fourier transform decomposes signals into frequencies. Eigenvalues represent observable quantities. Hilbert spaces provide the framework for quantum states. Group theory classifies fundamental particles. The calculus of variations yields equations of motion. These mathematical tools bridge abstract algebra and concrete physical phenomena.
