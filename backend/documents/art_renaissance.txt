The Renaissance was a cultural movement that began in Italy in the 14th century and spread across Europe. It marked the transition from the Middle Ages to modernity. Humanism emphasized human potential and achievements. Artists like Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, and Raphael created masterpieces that achieved new levels of realism and emotional expression. Linear perspective was developed to create depth in paintings. Oil painting techniques allowed for richer colors and finer detail. Sculptures such as David by Michelangelo celebrated the human form. Architecture revived classical Roman and Greek principles. The printing press, invented by Gutenberg, democratized knowledge. Science and art flourished as patrons funded exploration and innovation. The Renaissance produced works that continue to define Western artistic achievement.
