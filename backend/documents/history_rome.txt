The Roman Empire was one of the largest empires in history, spanning Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East. It began as a monarchy in 753 BC, became a republic in 509 BC, and transitioned to an empire under Augustus in 27 BC. Roman law, engineering, and architecture have had lasting influence. Aqueducts transported water across vast distances. Legionaries formed the backbone of the Roman military. The empire split into Western and Eastern halves; the Western part fell in 476 AD, while the Eastern Byzantine Empire survived until 1453. Latin, the Roman language, evolved into the Romance languages. Roman concrete allowed construction of enduring structures like the Pantheon. Slavery was a fundamental institution. Gladiatorial games were popular entertainment. Roman roads facilitated trade and military movement. The Roman Senate represented an early form of representative governance.
