Quantum mechanics describes the behavior of particles at the subatomic level. It introduces concepts like superposition, where a particle can exist in multiple states simultaneously, and entanglement, where particles become correlated in ways that classical physics cannot explain. The uncertainty principle states that certain pairs of physical properties cannot be known simultaneously with arbitrary precision. Quantum computing leverages qubits, which can represent both 0 and 1 at the same time, enabling parallel computation. Wave function collapse occurs upon measurement. Quantum field theory unifies quantum mechanics with special relativity. The observer effect suggests that the act of observation influences the system being observed. Quantum algorithms like Shor's algorithm can factor large numbers exponentially faster than classical computers. Quantum cryptography uses principles of quantum mechanics to secure communication.
