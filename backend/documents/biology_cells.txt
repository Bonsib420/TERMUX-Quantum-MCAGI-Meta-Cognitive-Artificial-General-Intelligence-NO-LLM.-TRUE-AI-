Cells are the basic units of life. All living organisms are composed of cells, from single-celled bacteria to complex multicellular organisms like humans. There are two main types of cells: prokaryotic cells (lacking a nucleus) and eukaryotic cells (with a nucleus). Eukaryotic cells contain organelles such as mitochondria (the powerhouses of the cell), endoplasmic reticulum, Golgi apparatus, and lysosomes. The cell membrane regulates what enters and exits the cell. DNA stores genetic information and is transcribed into RNA, which is then translated into proteins by ribosomes. Photosynthesis in plant cells converts sunlight into chemical energy. Cellular respiration produces ATP, the energy currency of the cell. osmosis and diffusion are mechanisms for transporting substances across membranes. Cell division allows growth and repair; mitosis produces identical daughter cells, while meiosis creates gametes for sexual reproduction.
