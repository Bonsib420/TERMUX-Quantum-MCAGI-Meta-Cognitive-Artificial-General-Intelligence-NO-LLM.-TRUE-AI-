Cognitive psychology studies mental processes such as perception, memory, reasoning, and problem-solving. It emerged in the mid-20th century as a reaction to behaviorism. Information processing models compare the mind to a computer. Attention determines what information gets processed. Short-term memory holds limited information briefly; long-term memory stores knowledge for extended periods. Schemas are mental frameworks that organize understanding. Cognitive biases, such as confirmation bias and anchoring, lead to systematic errors in judgment. Language acquisition involves innate structures according to Chomsky. Intelligence is measured by IQ tests but remains controversial. Cognitive neuroscience links mental processes to brain structures using imaging techniques. Problem-solving involves heuristics and algorithms. Decision-making often relies on heuristics like availability and representativeness.
