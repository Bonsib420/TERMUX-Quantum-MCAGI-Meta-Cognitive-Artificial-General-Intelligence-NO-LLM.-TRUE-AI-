Economics studies how societies allocate scarce resources. Microeconomics analyzes individual agents and markets. Supply and demand determine prices in a market economy. Elasticity measures responsiveness to price changes. Firms maximize profits by choosing optimal output levels. Market structures range from perfect competition to monopoly. Macroeconomics examines the economy as a whole, including GDP, inflation, and unemployment. Monetary policy uses interest rates and money supply to influence economic activity; central banks like the Federal Reserve implement it. Fiscal policy involves government spending and taxation. International trade allows countries to specialize according to comparative advantage. Exchange rates affect the price of goods across borders. Behavioral economics incorporates psychological insights into economic models. Market failures occur when free markets allocate resources inefficiently, justifying government intervention.
