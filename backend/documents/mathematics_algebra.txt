Algebra is the branch of mathematics dealing with symbols and the rules for manipulating them. It generalizes arithmetic by using variables to represent numbers. Linear equations describe straight lines and have the form ax + b = 0. Quadratic equations produce parabolas and are solved using the quadratic formula. Polynomials are sums of terms with variables raised to powers. Functions map inputs to outputs; they can be injective, surjective, or bijective. Groups, rings, and fields are algebraic structures studied in abstract algebra. Matrices represent linear transformations and can be added, multiplied, and inverted. Eigenvalues and eigenvectors reveal the intrinsic properties of matrices. Number theory explores properties of integers, including primes and modular arithmetic. Calculus extends algebra to continuous change through limits, derivatives, and integrals.
