Philosophy of mind examines the nature of consciousness and mental states. It questions whether the mind is identical to the brain or something distinct. Dualism argues that mental and physical substances are separate. Materialism claims everything is physical, including consciousness. The hard problem of consciousness asks why we have subjective experiences. Qualia refer to the raw feels of experience, like the redness of red or the pain of a headache. Intentionality is the mind's ability to represent things and states of affairs. Free will debates concern whether our choices are truly free or determined by prior causes. Phenomenology studies conscious experience from the first-person perspective. The mind-body problem explores how physical processes in the brain give rise to subjective awareness. Some propose that quantum processes in microtubules might explain consciousness (Orch-OR theory).
