Climate science studies Earth's weather patterns and long-term atmospheric conditions. Global average temperature is rising due to increased greenhouse gas emissions from burning fossil fuels. Carbon dioxide traps heat in the atmosphere, enhancing the greenhouse effect. Climate change leads to more extreme weather events, sea level rise, and ecosystem disruption. Renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and geothermal offer alternatives to fossil fuels. Deforestation reduces carbon sinks and biodiversity. International agreements like the Paris Agreement aim to limit warming. Adaptation strategies include building resilient infrastructure. Mitigation involves reducing emissions and enhancing carbon sequestration. Polar ice caps are melting, contributing to sea level rise. Ocean acidification harms marine life. Climate feedback loops can accelerate change, such as permafrost thaw releasing methane.
